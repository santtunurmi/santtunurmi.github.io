Bio

Through my education in JAMK as an information and communication technology engineer, I have studied a wide range of skills in tech. My specialization is in data-analytics and AI, but I strongly believe, that having a wide range of skills and knowledge is vital for any person to have. You can often find ways for how different things can be used together. I have maintained a steady interest in studying these skills in my own free time, not just within school hours. In a constantly developing world, with the tech industry developing even more rapidly, I think it's important, to stay up to date and brush up on your skills regularly.

Growing up in Äänekoski, Finland, now based in Jyväskylä, Finland, I find myself to be firmly grounded in my Finnish nationality. The values that I uphold are the sense of equality and comradery, which I learnt from growing up in the literal centre of Finland. This is juxtaposed to my love for computers, video games and the internet, from a very young age. Through my hobbies, mainly on the internet, I have got to meet many great people from outside of Finland. This has given me valuable perspective, that sometimes challenges the notions that live in the Finnish mentality.

Like any person from Finland, I will drone on and on about how great our country is, but through that background, I treat everyone with the sense of respect, that I would want myself to be treated with. From my experience, and from hearing people that I've had the plasure to have worked with, people see me as someone who is able to work together with people from many different backgrounds. I consider that important in an age, where we are increasingly divided.

In my teenage years I worked several "summer jobs" to get work experience. It is quite common in Finland to do a short one-month internship at a local grocery store, for example, as you are not allowed to work even part-time as a teenager, but a short summer job can still give you some valuable work experience and own money. Pair that with me growing up working class and my mom owning a small local business, which she also ran by working there full-time, I had plenty of influence on my relationship to work.

One of these key lessons was the fact, that in all of the places that I worked at, the manager would do work as everyone else. Often better and more efficient than everyone else, too. The fact that you see your boss working right there beside you gives you the confidence and respect of their leadership. It makes it obvious, that they know the work inside-and-out, and can use that knowledge to make better decisions, when managing the bigger picture. In addition, my coworkers were always encouraging, open to help, and most of all, respected me as one of their colleagues, despite of the fact, that I was just a summer intern.

This left an impression on me, and it's one of the core values, that I wish from any future employer. I believe it's not just for my benefit, but the work culture as a whole will be a lot more open and focused, leading to a better business.

After high school, it was crystal clear to me, that I wanted to choose my higher education to be in the tech industry. This was largely because I have always been interested in computers, internet and tech, but also due to me wanting to pursue a more hands-on field in academia.

Currently I am on course for finishing my degree in 2026. I have always been above average as a student, but I am more focused on projects and innovation, rather than finding my passion in learning to simply learn. I have my curiosities, and often they are the impetus to my projects and ideas, but I prefer to create something tangible with my efforts, rather than to build up skills just to show them off for validation.
