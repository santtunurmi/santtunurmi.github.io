My thoughts on generative AI:

As stated previously, I believe that when it comes to AI, and generative AI especially, one should be cautious on it's utilization. I have no illusions about potential sci-fi scenarios happening. Generative AI is not sentient. What I am far more concerned about, is the rapid development and utilization of generative AI in the form of large language models, which are quickly being adapated at large, without addressing several key issues in their functionality, as well as the ethical implications of their use. Examples of this include chatbot models such as ChatGPT, but particularly in image generation models, such as Stable Diffusion and Midjourney, as well as music generation models like Riffusion.

Generative AI has had wide-spread effects on the entertainment and creative industries, as a combination of audience disinterest, and the potential for corporations to save money on asset and content creation, generative AI has the very real threat to eliminate human creatives, through taking up the monetary support and resources available in the industry. One could argue, that in order for art to evolve, it would still require original, talented human artists, as at the moment, generative AI cannot conceive anything truly original. This is because the content being generated by generative AI models is based on the data being fed in, which is art that already exists.

It has been said, that the existence of art, is what seperates us from animals.
